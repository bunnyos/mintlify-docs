BunnyOS signs you in with your Base wallet. There's no email, no password, and no separate username step.

## Connect your wallet

On the landing page you'll see a **`connect to base`** button. Clicking it opens a small popup where you approve the connection on Base. That's the entire flow.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click connect to base">
    On the BunnyOS landing page, click the **`connect to base`** button. A confirmation sound plays and the button briefly shows **`connecting…`** while the popup opens.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve in the popup">
    A popup window opens against Base's sign-in page. Approve the connection there.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Land in the terminal">
    Once you approve, the popup reports success and BunnyOS sends you straight into the terminal, already signed in.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  If you're already signed in and revisit the landing page, the button reads **`open terminal`** instead and takes you back into the app, no need to reconnect.
</Note>

<Warning>
  Your browser must allow the popup to open. If popups are blocked for the BunnyOS site, the button quietly returns to **`connect to base`** without starting the flow. Allow popups and click again.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  Signing in never asks for a seed phrase, never requires a transaction, and never exposes your private keys to BunnyOS. Approving the connection grants BunnyOS a session, not custody of your wallet.
</Tip>

## Staying signed in

After you connect, BunnyOS keeps you signed in so you don't have to reconnect every visit. If your Base connection later drops, for example after a long idle period or if you remove BunnyOS from Base, the **`connect to base`** button comes back so you can reconnect.

## Connection status and signing out

The terminal's top bar shows a small status dot on the right:

| Indicator | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Green dot | BunnyOS is connected and healthy |
| Red dot | BunnyOS can't connect right now |

When you're signed in, a **`sign out`** button appears in the top bar next to your status. Clicking it signs you out fully.

<Warning>
  Signing out fully disconnects your Base session, it doesn't just hide the UI. The next time you want back in, you'll go through **`connect to base`** again.
</Warning>
