---
title: "Getting data: managed vs your own keys"
description: "Choose between the managed bunnyCompute option (zero keys) or bringing your own provider keys."
---

BunnyOS needs outside data to act on your behalf: **market and security data** (token prices, trending, wallet history, portfolio positions, token security) and the **AI** that powers the agent's reasoning. You can supply both in one of two ways, and you switch between them in the app.

## Two ways to power the agent

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="bunnyCompute (managed)" icon="zap" href="/user/bunnyds/overview">
    Data and AI are handled for you by BunnyOS and billed to your wallet in USDC. Connect your wallet and approve an allowance. Free until metering is enabled.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Bring your own keys" icon="key" href="/user/configure/api-keys">
    You paste your own provider keys and pick your own AI provider. Full control, but you manage the accounts and credits.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  You don't have to pick one forever. A key you supply yourself always takes priority for that provider, so you can mix and match.
</Note>

## bunnyCompute (managed), recommended

This is the simplest path and the one most users want. On the **bunny compute services** page, flip **bunnyCompute active** on and the agent's market data and AI route through the managed gateway. Then connect your wallet and approve a USDC allowance so usage can be billed to you. See [the bunnyCompute overview](/user/bunnyds/overview) for the full walkthrough.

While bunnyCompute is on, you don't need to set up most provider keys, and the AI-provider and model sections in the **services** tab are hidden.

<Tip>
  bunnyCompute is **free until metering is enabled** on the gateway. While it's free, the spending allowance section stays locked and you pay nothing. Once metering is on, you approve a USDC allowance and pay only for what the agent uses. See [Spending and billing](/user/bunnyds/spending).
</Tip>

## Bring your own keys

If you're running the open-source build, or you simply prefer to use your own accounts, you can supply each provider key yourself. Open [Configure](/user/configure/api-keys) and use the **services** tab. There you'll find:

- An **AI provider** group (marked `*required`) where you paste your provider key, then pick the provider and choose a model in the same **services** tab.
- Keys for market and wallet data providers.

Each key has a **set api** button with a link to grab a key from the provider, and a dot that turns green once a key is configured. A green dot means "configured"; an empty dot means "no key set." Keys you enter yourself can be cleared at any time.

<Note>
  At minimum, this needs an AI provider key (the `*required` one) plus the data keys for the providers you want the agent to use. See [Configuration: API keys](/user/configure/api-keys) for what each key unlocks.
</Note>

## How to switch

<Steps>
  <Step title="Turn bunnyCompute on (managed)">
    Open the **bunny compute services** page and flip the switch to **bunnyCompute active**. The agent uses the managed option right away, and your own key fields are hidden in Configure.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Turn bunnyCompute off (your own keys)">
    Flip the same switch off. The page note then reads that data and AI "use your own keys from the configure tab," and the provider and key sections reappear in the **services** tab of [Configure](/user/configure/api-keys).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set your keys">
    With bunnyCompute off, open the **services** tab in Configure, paste your AI provider key, then add any data keys, then pick your AI provider and choose a model in the same tab and you're ready.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What each unlocks

| | bunnyCompute (managed) | Bring your own keys |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Setup | None, one switch | Paste a key per provider |
| Provider keys needed | Zero | You supply each one |
| AI provider | Handled for you | You pick and configure |
| Cost | Metered in USDC, free until enabled | Whatever your providers charge |
| Best for | Getting started fast | Open-source builds, full control |

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I use bunnyCompute for data but my own AI?">
    bunnyCompute covers both data and AI together, so there's no way to split them. Turn it off and set your keys in Configure to run your own AI provider.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens to keys I already set if I turn bunnyCompute on?">
    They're kept but not used. While bunnyCompute is active the agent uses the managed option, so your saved keys aren't used until you turn it off again.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
