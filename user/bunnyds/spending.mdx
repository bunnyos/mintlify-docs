When [bunnyCompute](/user/bunnyds/overview) is on, the agent's data and AI run through the managed gateway, and usage is metered against your wallet and settled in USDC on Base. This page covers the **spending allowance** you approve, the billing figures the page shows, and your **network usage** so far.

## How billing works

bunnyCompute is pay-as-you-go. You grant a **USDC allowance**, a cap on how much the gateway may settle from your wallet, and you pay only for what the agent actually uses. Your USDC stays in your own wallet until it is settled, so approving an allowance never moves funds on its own.

To have spendable credit you need two things: an allowance that is high enough, and a real USDC balance to back it. Your **credit** is whichever of those is smaller, minus what you already owe.

<Note>
  These spending controls are only about paying for data and AI. They are separate from your trading: you still need a connected wallet with its own funds to trade with the agent. See [Connect your wallet](/user/getting-started/wallet-connection).
</Note>

## Approve a spending allowance

The **spending allowance** card sits below the status and connect cards on the *bunny compute services* page.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter an amount">
    Type an amount in the **USDC allowance** field. This is the total USDC the gateway may settle over time, not a per-use charge.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve allowance">
    Choose **approve allowance** and approve the on-chain allowance in your Base wallet. Approving needs only your Base wallet to sign, so you can set an allowance without connecting first. After it confirms, your new allowance appears in the figures below. It can take up to a minute to update.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  To see your live billing figures, connect your wallet on the bunnyCompute page first. Approving an allowance works without connecting, but the allowance, balance, owed, and credit figures only appear once your wallet is connected. See [the bunnyCompute overview](/user/bunnyds/overview).
</Note>

<Tip>
  You can raise the cap any time by approving a new total. To stop bunnyCompute spending entirely, turn bunnyCompute off on the status card.
</Tip>

## Your billing figures

Once your wallet is connected, the card shows four live figures:

| Field | What it shows |
| --- | --- |
| allowance | The USDC cap you have approved for the gateway |
| balance | Your wallet's USDC balance |
| owed | Usage you have run up but not yet settled |
| credit | Your remaining spendable headroom |

Your **credit** is the smaller of your allowance and your balance, minus what you owe. When it reaches zero, billed requests stop until you raise the allowance or top up your USDC. A `—` means there is nothing to show yet.

## Network usage

Below the spending card, the **network usage** section totals what the agent has run through bunnyCompute:

| Figure | What it shows |
| --- | --- |
| total requests | How many requests the agent has sent to the gateway |
| total input tokens | Tokens sent to the AI model |
| total output tokens | Tokens the AI model produced |

These are running totals, so they let you see how much you are using at a glance, whether or not metering is on yet.

## When bunnyCompute is free

If metering is not enabled on the gateway, bunnyCompute runs free: the spending allowance card is greyed out with a note that metering is not enabled yet and no allowance is needed. You can turn bunnyCompute on and use data and AI at no cost, with no approval step.

<Warning>
  If bunnyCompute is not available on your deployment at all, bring your own provider keys instead. See [Getting data: managed vs your own keys](/user/getting-started/data-and-keys).
</Warning>

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Will I be charged for bunnyCompute?">
    Only when metering is enabled and you have approved an allowance. Even then, your USDC stays in your wallet until the gateway settles what you actually used, and never beyond the allowance you set. While bunnyCompute is free, nothing is billed.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Why can't I enter an allowance?">
    The card is locked while metering is not enabled on the gateway. There is nothing to cap yet, so the field and the approve button stay off until metering goes live.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do I stop spending?">
    Turn bunnyCompute off on the status card. That stops the gateway from settling anything further.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="My new allowance or balance hasn't updated">
    The on-chain figures are read on a short delay and refresh roughly every 15 seconds. Give a new approval up to a minute to appear.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
