description: "Turn on bunnyCompute to run BunnyOS data and AI through the managed gateway, billed to your wallet in USDC."
---

bunnyCompute lets you use the full BunnyOS terminal without setting up any API keys of your own. When it's on, BunnyOS routes the agent's market data, token security, and AI through its **managed gateway**, and usage is metered against your wallet in USDC on Base. There's nothing to install: turn it on, connect your wallet, approve a spending allowance, and the agent is ready.

<Note>
  bunnyCompute only charges once metering is enabled on the gateway. Until then it runs free and no allowance is needed. See [Spending & billing](/user/bunnyds/spending).
</Note>

## What you get when bunnyCompute is on

With bunnyCompute active, BunnyOS supplies what the agent needs to read markets and reason, with no personal keys required:

| Capability | What it covers |
| --- | --- |
| Market & token data | Prices, markets, trending, and on-chain pool data |
| Portfolio & wallet data | Your holdings and wallet reads for the portfolio dashboard |
| Token security | Risk and rug checks on Base tokens |
| AI | The agent's chat and reasoning, on the managed model |

<Note>
  A few providers still use your own key even with bunnyCompute on. If a data source on the **configure** page keeps its **set api** button while bunnyCompute is active, paste a key there to enable it. See [Configuration: API keys](/user/configure/api-keys).
</Note>

<Note>
  While bunnyCompute is on, the AI-provider and model controls in the **configure** tab are greyed out, because BunnyOS supplies the model for you.
</Note>

## The bunny compute services page

Open the **bunnyCompute** tab, titled *bunny compute services* ("bunnyOS managed data + inference gateway"). The page stacks three cards top to bottom:

1. **Status**: the on/off switch for bunnyCompute.
2. **Connect your wallet**: sign in so usage can be billed to your wallet.
3. **Spending allowance**: approve how much USDC bunnyCompute may settle.

### 1. Turn bunnyCompute on

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the page and read the status">
    A colored dot and label show the current state. A green dot with **bunnyCompute active** means data and inference route through the managed gateway. A grey dot with **bunnyCompute off** means the agent uses your own keys from the configure tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Flip the toggle">
    Use the switch at the top right of the status card. The status updates as soon as the change is saved.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  If a save fails, you'll see a brief "couldn't update bunnyCompute, try again" message and the toggle returns to its previous position. Just try again.
</Warning>

### 2. Connect your wallet

Because usage is billed per wallet, bunnyCompute needs to know which wallet is paying. In the **connect your wallet** card, choose **connect wallet** and sign the message in your Base wallet. This mints a bunnyCompute session token bound to that wallet, and usage is billed to it. Once connected, the card reads **wallet connected**, and a **reconnect** button lets you sign again if your session expires.

<Note>
  This signature is only proof that you control the wallet. It does not move any funds. Approving the [spending allowance](/user/bunnyds/spending) is the step that authorizes payment.
</Note>

### 3. Approve a spending allowance

In the **spending allowance** card, enter an amount of USDC and choose **approve allowance**, then approve the on-chain allowance in your Base wallet. The allowance is a cap: your USDC stays in your wallet, and the gateway only settles what you actually use. You can raise it any time. For the full billing picture, see [Spending & billing](/user/bunnyds/spending).

The page also shows a **network usage** section with your total requests and total input/output tokens. For what those mean and how they tie into billing, see [Spending & billing](/user/bunnyds/spending).

## bunnyCompute vs. your own keys

Turning bunnyCompute **off** means you bring your own keys instead. For when to use each option, see [Getting data: managed vs your own keys](/user/getting-started/data-and-keys).
