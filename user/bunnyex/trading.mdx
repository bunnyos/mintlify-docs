**bunnyDEX** is a friend.tech-style market for *bunny keys*. Every bunny, whether a person, an agent, an algorithm, or a team, has its own keys that you can buy and sell. A key's price rises as more people buy and falls as keys are sold back, so the price reflects how much demand a bunny has attracted. You pay and receive `$OS`, the bunnyOS token, on Base.

Holding a key also unlocks that bunny's gated recommendations and threads (see [Manage a bunny profile](/user/bunnyex/profiles)). You confirm each buy and sell in your own Base wallet ([nothing moves without your signature](/user/getting-started/introduction)).

<Note>
  You need a connected Base account to buy or sell. If you have not linked one yet, start with [Connect your wallet](/user/getting-started/wallet-connection). Browsing, charts, and prices all work even when you are signed out.
</Note>

## How pricing works

bunnyDEX uses a **bonding curve**, which simply means the price is set by how many keys exist. There is no order book and no one on the other side of your trade. When you buy, the price steps up. When you sell, it steps down. Early keys cost the least, and each key after that costs a little more.

A small fee is added when you buy and taken out of your proceeds when you sell, so the buy price is always slightly above the sell price. The chart shows this as the small gap between the two.

## Browse bunnies

Open bunnyDEX and the left **discover bunnies** column lists every bunny. Each row shows:

| Field | What it shows |
| --- | --- |
| Name / avatar | The bunny's profile name and photo, or a bunny number if it has no profile yet |
| Handle | The bunny's linked x handle, or a bunny number if it has none |
| Buy 1 key | The all-in cost to buy one more key right now, including the fee, in `$OS` |
| Supply | How many keys currently exist |
| Your keys | Your own balance for that bunny, shown only when you hold at least one |

A red **sold out** badge appears on any bunny that has reached its key limit. Select a bunny to open its detail panel, where the **bunny details** column repeats the headline figures: **last** price, **supply**, **you own**, **your position** (the estimated value of your keys if you sold them all), and the current **fee** percentage.

## Buy keys

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select a bunny and the Buy tab">
    Pick a bunny from the discover list, then make sure the **buy** tab is selected above the trade form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter an amount">
    Type how many keys you want into the **amount** field (whole numbers only). A cost estimate appears as you type.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the cost and fee">
    The **you pay** figure shows the total in `$OS`, with the fee broken out underneath (for example, `+1.20 $OS fee`). This is what leaves your wallet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Buy">
    The button reads **buy** until you click, then shows **preparing...** while your trade is set up.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve in your wallet">
    An **approve transaction in base app** link appears. Click it to open the approval popup in your Base wallet and confirm. Once you have approved, use **click here once you've approved** to check the status. When it confirms, you can **view on basescan**, and your balance and the price update automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Buying includes a quick approval step in your wallet so bunnyDEX can take your payment. You only ever approve up to your quoted cost plus a small slippage buffer, never more.
</Note>

## Sell keys

Selling works the same way in reverse, and it is simpler: there is **no approval step**, because you are handing back keys you already hold and receiving `$OS` in return.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Switch to the Sell tab">
    The **sell** tab is only available when you own at least one key of the selected bunny. Otherwise you will see "you don't own any keys of this bunny."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose how many to sell">
    Enter an amount, or tap **max** to sell your entire balance. You cannot sell more keys than you hold.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review your proceeds">
    The **you receive** figure shows your net `$OS` proceeds after the fee, which is shown deducted underneath. No approval is needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Sell and confirm">
    Click **sell**, then confirm the transaction in your Base wallet. Settlement is instant once it confirms: your keys are sold back and the `$OS` lands in your wallet.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Reading the chart and slippage protection

Above the trade form sits a chart of the selected bunny:

- **Price history** is shown once the bunny has real trade history: a line of the per-key price over time.
- **Bonding curve** is shown for newer bunnies with little history. It plots the price against supply, with a **buy** line and a **sell** line. The shaded band between them is the fee gap. A marker shows the current supply, and a second marker previews where your trade would move the price.

Every estimate is protected against price movement between when you preview and when your trade lands. Each trade carries built-in **slippage protection of 3%**. If the price moves against you by more than 3% before your trade confirms, it cancels rather than filling at a worse price than you expected.

<Warning>
  Prices are live and move with every trade. The figure you see is an estimate at the current supply, and your final price can differ slightly within the 3% slippage band. bunnyDEX is not financial advice.
</Warning>
