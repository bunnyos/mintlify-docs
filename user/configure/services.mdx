The agent can connect to price feeds, wallet data, and market intelligence. The **Services** tab in [Configure](/user/configure/api-keys) is where you decide which ones are switched on. Anything you turn off is hidden from the agent and never used. Enabled services are offered to the agent on every turn.

## The Services tab

Open **Configure** and select the **services** tab. You'll see services grouped into a few sections:

- **mcp**: the connection that lets the agent read your balances and build send, swap, and sign transactions for you to approve.
- **bunny core**: the main data sources, on by default.
- **bunny optional**: extra sources that are **off by default**, so you opt in only to what you need.
- **x402 marketplace**: pay-per-call microservices the agent can browse and call (see below).
- **notifications**: the **telegram push** toggle (see below).
- **security**: **action security**, which is always on (see below).

Each row has a label, a short status line, and a toggle on the right. Click a row to open that service in its own tab.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the service">
    Scroll to the right section. The status line tells you its state: how many tools it offers, off, not authorized, or offline.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Flip the toggle">
    Use the switch on the right of the row to turn the service on or off. It saves right away, with no separate save step.
  </Step>
  <Step title="(Optional) set a key">
    Services that need their own key show a **set api** button. Managed ones show **powered by bunnyCompute** instead.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Some services need their own key (**moralis**, **coingecko**, **gmgn**). **coingecko** is required, since the price data almost every other tool relies on flows through it. When [bunnyCompute](/user/bunnyds/overview) is on it covers these for you and they show **powered by bunnyCompute** instead of a key button. See [Getting data: managed vs your own keys](/user/getting-started/data-and-keys).
</Note>

## Why turn services off

Turning off services you don't use keeps the agent focused and avoids unnecessary calls on any provider key you supply yourself. Some optional sources overlap with what the core sources already cover, which is why they ship off by default. Turn them on only when you want their extra data.

<Tip>
  A good default is to leave **bunny core** on and only switch on a **bunny optional** service when you have a concrete reason. Trade execution stays opt-in: the agent only builds transactions, and you sign every one in your own wallet ([how this works](/user/getting-started/wallet-connection)).
</Tip>

## Action security

**Action security** sits in the **security** section. It is **always on** and cannot be turned off. Every action is screened before it reaches your [inbox](/user/actions/inbox), whether it came from your own agent or from a bunny whose keys you hold.

It first strips disguises (unicode tricks, hidden characters, odd spacing, leetspeak, even morse) so hidden attacks still get caught, then blocks anything that trips a rule:

- **Wallet drains**: transfers or swaps that would move your whole balance to someone else.
- **Unlimited approvals**: unbounded token approvals that would let a contract spend your funds freely.
- **Secret exfiltration**: attempts to leak your private key, seed phrase, or session.
- **Prompt injection**: jailbreak or hidden instructions buried inside an action.

Anything that trips a rule is dropped silently and never reaches you.

## x402 marketplace

The **x402 marketplace** lets the agent browse and call pay-per-call microservices. Each call is paid directly from your own Base wallet in **USDC**. Enable a marketplace in this section to let the agent use its services. Like the rest of trade execution, spend stays in your hands: payment comes from your wallet, not a managed balance.

## Telegram push

Turn on **telegram push** in the **notifications** section to get your [action alerts](/user/actions/inbox) in Telegram.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a bot">
    Open **@BotFather** in Telegram and create a bot, then copy the bot token it gives you.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste the token">
    Paste the token into the **bot token** field on the services tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Detect your chat id">
    Open your new bot in Telegram and send it any message, then click **detect** to fill in your chat id automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and test">
    Click **save**, then **test** to send yourself a test message and confirm it's working.
  </Step>
</Steps>
